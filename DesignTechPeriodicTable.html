Exploring Capabilities of Laser-Cutter

Each of the 18 columns that make up the periodic table (plus 1 blank column) were digitally created using CorelDraw software, and then laser cut on 7/32" plywood. The elements include the name, symbol, and atomic weight and number. Additionally, elements were color-coded based on their groupings. As for the bottom two rows of the periodic table (the lanthanides and actinides), these were included on the seven seats around the table.

Integrating 3D-Printed Technology

Students created a trivia-themed game centered around the periodic table and chemistry in general. Each of the game pieces was designed using Google SketchUp and then printed on a 3D Makerbot printer.

Repurposing Materials

After a long search for an old cable spool, the student were able to finally locate one through the help of the school system's maintenance crew. The spool required a few minor modifications such as supports for stability, height adjustment, and installation of a lazy-susan apparatus.
